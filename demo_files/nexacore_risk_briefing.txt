COMPANY OVERVIEW
----------------
NexaCore Technologies is a mid-size electronics manufacturer headquartered in Munich, Germany.
We produce industrial IoT sensors and edge computing modules sold to automotive, healthcare,
and smart infrastructure clients across Europe and North America.

Annual Revenue: $340M
Employees: 2,400
Manufacturing Sites: Munich (Germany), Wroclaw (Poland)
Primary Markets: Germany (35%), USA (28%), France (14%), UK (12%), Other (11%)


CRITICAL DEPENDENCIES & KNOWN VULNERABILITIES
----------------------------------------------

1. SEMICONDUCTOR CONCENTRATION RISK — CRITICAL
   Our primary source for advanced 5nm processing chips is TSMC in Taiwan (Supplier SUP-001).
   TSMC accounts for 78% of our chip procurement. No qualified alternative source exists for
   the 5nm node. A 3-month disruption to TSMC operations would halt our flagship product line
   (NexaSense Pro) within 6 weeks due to our current safety stock levels.

   Current safety stock: 6 weeks at current production rate.
   Qualification lead time for alternative (Intel Fab Ireland): estimated 14-18 months.

2. RED SEA SHIPPING DISRUPTION — HIGH (ONGOING)
   All maritime shipments from Asia to our Munich and Wroclaw facilities travel via the
   Suez Canal route. Since Q4 2024, Houthi attacks in the Red Sea have forced our carriers
   to reroute via the Cape of Good Hope, adding 14-18 days to transit times and increasing
   freight costs by approximately €1.2M per quarter vs. baseline.

   Impact: Component inventory buffers strained. Production planning horizon extended from
   6 weeks to 10 weeks. Air freight used for urgent semiconductor shipments (high cost).

3. UKRAINE WIRING HARNESSES — HIGH
   Supplier SUP-007 (Flex Ltd Ukraine) produces 100% of our proprietary wiring harnesses
   used in the NexaCore Edge series. This is a single-source supplier with a factory in
   Lviv, Western Ukraine. Contract expires August 2025. Last audit score: 72/100.

   Continuity: Factory has not experienced direct disruption however insurance underwriters
   have flagged the Romania relocation option as mandatory for contract renewal.
   We have begun qualification of an alternative supplier in Bialystok, Poland (18 months
   to qualify).

4. LITHIUM SUPPLY CHAIN — MEDIUM
   Battery cells sourced from Samsung SDI (South Korea). Raw lithium sourced from Albemarle
   (Chile) and Rio Tinto (Australia). No single-source dependency at raw material level.
   However, lithium hydroxide price volatility is significant (+40% YoY in 2025).

5. CURRENCY & TARIFF RISK — MEDIUM
   28% of our revenue comes from the US market, invoiced in USD. EUR/USD volatility has
   compressed margins by approximately 2.1% in Q1 2026.
   New US Section 301 tariff review scheduled for June 2026 may affect electronic components
   imported from China (currently 15% tariff). We have begun dual-sourcing PCB assembly
   to Vietnam (SUP-004) to reduce China exposure.


RECENT INCIDENTS
----------------
- March 2026: TSMC fab in Hsinchu experienced a 2-day power outage. Impact to NexaCore:
  delayed one production batch, approximately €800K revenue impact. Recovery complete.
- February 2026: Brazilian port workers' strike (Santos) delayed nickel sulphate shipment
  from Vale by 9 days. Safety stock absorbed the delay.
- January 2026: Ukrainian supplier Flex Ltd flagged potential factory relocation by Q3 2026.
  NexaCore procurement team engaged in emergency qualification of alternative.


STRATEGIC PRIORITIES FOR Q2/Q3 2026
--------------------------------------
1. Reduce TSMC single-source dependency below 50% by end of 2027.
2. Dual-source all Tier-1 suppliers for NexaSense Pro product line.
3. Increase safety stock for critical semiconductors from 6 weeks to 12 weeks.
4. Establish nearshore European suppliers for Tier-2 wiring and assembly components.
5. Implement dynamic re-routing protocol for maritime shipments (trigger: >7 day delay).
